## 2. The .tmf OUTCOME  `[RD-0114-O1]`

The .tmf is a digital, fail-closed **signed capability passport** — the CONTROL PLANE of "Govern, Don't Absorb" — and it is **real today on bit-exact silicon**: a SHAKE256 3-ary Merkle integrity root, a fail-closed container, KEM-DEM confidentiality, an anti-rollback history chain with §5 freshness, and the `admitPhotonicConfig` 4-gate rail that admits/denies a substrate config blob T (hash-pin + Ed25519 + revocation + capability) collapsing to a K3 verdict (unknown→deny). The custody/capsule specs are frozen v0, byte-reproducible, Blocked only on wiring a vetted FIPS-204 lib (`@noble/post-quantum` already a dep). **Position it as the trust/admission ENVELOPE that governs a substrate blob: it carries T's signed bytes and decides admission digitally; it never absorbs the analog physics and never routes integrity through optics.** The 52-3D claims do not change what the .tmf *is* — they describe a future substrate it would *govern*.

## 3. The DB OUTCOME  `[RD-0114-O2]`

The TritMesh DB is, and should be positioned as, a **digital Data-Oriented-Design database on binary silicon**: SoA storage, a genuinely-shipped O(1) bump/arena allocator (with per-flow reset + secret-zero), cache-packed scans, and a classical O(log N) indexed search over digital SHA-256 CIDs. That is the real, citable engine — and the O(1)/SoA/index win is a **constant-factor / latency-predictability** story (Gay & Aiken 1998 region allocation; Galerina's net-new is *governing* the arena ceiling, B1 closing the prior 8 MB-declared / 128 MB-shipped fail-open), **not an asymptotic miracle**. Every quantum/photonic DB claim either fails or is unbuilt: Grover REFUTED as an engine; holographic "O(1)-read petabyte" overstated→refuted; photonic tensor offload latency-O(1)/work-Θ(N²), ~1.9× after ADC/DAC → at most an HW-gated Tri-Pipe co-processor for large batched ANN. **Position as "fast because the data layout and the index are good"; treat any substrate as a signed, governed blob; the record integrity anchor stays bit-exact digital.**

## 4. The COMPARISON — the decisive insight  `[RD-0114-O3]`

**Hypothesis CONFIRMED, with one sharpening.** Both sound parts are DIGITAL; the two outcomes are the two halves of one architecture split along the **control-plane / data-plane** line:

- The **.tmf is the CONTROL PLANE**, and its net-new value *is* governance — being a signed passport is the whole point. Its soundness is **constructive**: the value comes from a thing that genuinely exists and is useful (custody, admission, K3 verdicts). It **benefits** from the substrate conversation because it is the mechanism that admits the substrate.
- The **TritMesh DB is the DATA PLANE**, and its net-new value is **digital DoD engineering** (allocator + SoA + classical index). Its soundness is **defensive**: the photonic/quantum promises are refuted or aspirational, and the real engine is what survives once you strip them out. It is **threatened** by the substrate conversation — the hype, if absorbed, would replace a provably-better classical index with a provably-worse quantum one.

The unifying principle is **GOVERN, DON'T ABSORB**, and the comparison shows *why it cuts both ways from a single seam* — `admitPhotonicConfig`. The .tmf is the **passport**; the DB is the **passport-holder's home turf that stays digital**. The substrate (photonic processor, holographic store, quantum search) is in BOTH cases an **admitted co-processor, not a place data lives or a security primitive**. **Sharpening:** the photonic layer is identical in *role* (a HW-gated, governed accelerator/storage substrate) but **opposite in argumentative posture** — for the .tmf the substrate is the *object of governance* (the rail is the deliverable); for the DB the substrate is the *temptation to refute* (the digital engine is the deliverable). Same seam, same principle, mirror-image consequence. **The decisive insight: the .tmf's win is that governance is real; the DB's win is that the hype is refuted and the digital engine is what's left standing.**

## 5. GUARDRAILS — two things that must NEVER be designed in

**Guardrail A `[RD-0114-G1]` — NEVER a MAC-less .tmf.** The format must ALWAYS keep its digital hybrid Ed25519 + ML-DSA-65 signature over the 32-byte integrity root (SPORE-SUBSTRATE-001 + custody §1: "never replace the hash with a signature, never use a signature as an address/key"). A MAC's security is EUF-CMA keyed binding (Boneh–Zhandry 2013); entanglement/QBER senses channel disturbance, not payload authenticity — no quantum advantage over classical MACs. A QBER/`toleranceWitness` may be CARRIED as an OPTIONAL DEGRADE-ONLY K3 field (`E = min(T_digital, T_physical)` — can only lower a verdict, never manufacture ALLOW), HW-gated behind real hardware + attested calibrated ε + an authenticated classical channel. Integrity anchor stays bit-exact digital SHAKE256/SHA-256. Unknown/no-attestation → INDETERMINATE → deny.

**Guardrail B `[RD-0114-G2]` — NEVER a Grover/holographic-dependent DB engine.** Record/CID integrity and the search path stay classical-digital. Grover/QRAM must never be the search path or in any complexity/perf claim — at realistic N a classical B-tree/CSS-tree index (O(log N) over digital CIDs) provably wins. The index is the default; any photonic tensor path is opt-in, large-batched-ANN-only, behind a measured ADC/DAC budget. Any storage/search/index substrate is admitted only as a SIGNED blob through `admitPhotonicConfig`. **Banned claim language:** "O(1) quantum DB search," "O(1)-read petabyte storage," "photonic database," any √N/Grover speedup figure, MAC-free integrity. **Allowed:** "O(1) bump allocator, SoA cache-locality, O(log N) classical index."

**Guardrail C `[RD-0114-G3]` — NEVER overwrite-erasure on a write-once/fixed substrate (sound-erasure obligation, `SPORE-RETAIN-001`).** Galerina's secret-erasure is overwrite-based (`memset` the arena / derived-secret buffer; B2/B2b). On write-once/fixed media (thermally-fixed holograms can't be erased optically; WORM glass is immutable) that invariant is **silently false** — the only sound erasure is **crypto-erase** (NIST SP 800-88 "Purge": destroy the key). So any substrate flagged `eraseModel: "crypto-only"` must receive **only KEM-DEM ciphertext** (a cleartext-secret-tainted value reaching it is a fail-closed error, extending SPORE-SECRET-002 / SPORE-PRIVACY-002), the DEK lives on overwritable digital silicon, "deletion" = key destruction, and erasure emits a key-destruction **witness**. See [`RD-0116`](galerina-rd-0116-holographic-storage-2026-06-24.md). Build gated on a storage-admission path (#102-106); the obligation is recorded now so it cannot be silently violated.
